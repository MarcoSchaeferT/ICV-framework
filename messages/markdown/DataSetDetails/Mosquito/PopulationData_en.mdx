
##### GHS-POP Population Data
GHSL population grid (POP) — a spatial raster dataset that depicts the distribution of residential population, expressed as the number of people per cell. Residential population estimates are derived from the raw global census data harmonized by CIESIN for the Gridded Population of the World, version 4.11 (GPWv4.11) at polygon level, and disaggregated from census or administrative units to grid cells, informed by the distribution, classification and volume of built-up as mapped in the GHSL global layers per corresponding epoch.

- **Spatial coverage/extent:** -180°, 90°, 180°, -90°
- **Temporal coverage:** 1975–2030
- **Spatial resolution:** 100 m, 1 km, 3 ss, 30 ss
- **Temporal resolution:** 5 years

*Source: Schiavina M., Freire S., Carioli A., MacManus K. (2023): GHS-POP R2023A – GHS population grid multitemporal (1975-2030). European Commission, Joint Research Centre (JRC)*
